CSCI 201 [1,3] Fall 2002

Programming Assignment 1

Due Tuesday, Oct. 1, 2002

Objective

This assignment is to provide you with experience analyzing a problem and performing the prescribed sequence of steps to develop an algorithm for its solution.

Problem

Design a program to abstractly model a vending machine.

You will provide (and turn in):
  • A set of clarifying questions with corresponding answers to precisely define, specify, and constrain your problem;
  • A structure chart exhibiting the functional relationships and hierarchies inherent in your design;
  • A pseudocode algorithm  to  model the functioning of the vending machine.
  • A walk-through of your algorithm with an instance of test data that exhibits the working of your vending machine.


    • Each page you turn in will be neat, bound with the other pages, and exhibit  your name, course, section, and date.

    Specification hints
    I suggest you use the following specifications in your design:
  • 2 products to be vended [output] (can have different prices)
  • coin slot [input](specify accepted coins)
  • selection buttons for each product [input]
  • selection button for change [input] (releases remaining balance back to the customer)
  • an informational display [output] (it will print a message to the customer)
  • sample questions:
  • if there are limits, what are they and what happens when you try to exceed them, e.g. running out of a product. or pressing a vending button before there's a sufficient balance to vend.
  • will you make change (refund balance) after a product has been vended, or only after the "change" button has been pushed?
    • Extra Credit
    Provide the pseudocode for detailing exactly how the balance will be refunded as change using the fewest possible (minimum number of) coins.
