Anecdotes

Ernest Miller Hemingway 1899-1961

Hemingway was a war correspondent during the Spanish Civil War and World War II. He was among the first to enter liberated Paris in 1944. It's said he "liberated" the wine cellar at the Ritz. When he stopped to greet Pablo Picasso, and learned the famous artist wasn't in, he left a wooden crate labeled "To Picasso From Hemingway." When the crate was opened it contained hand grenades.
