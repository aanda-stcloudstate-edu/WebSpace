Homework:

  1. Failures & successes paper. {Due Feb. 15}
    • List roughly a half-dozen to a dozen examples of technological software and/or hardware innovations and/or products which had no immediate impact.
      In each case, list the factors that contributed to their failure to be immediately influential or capture the marketplace.
    • Conversely, list roughly a half-dozen to a dozen examples of technological software and/or hardware products which had a profound and immediate impact despite being technologically equivalent at best to competing products.
      In each case, list the factors that contributed to their success.
    • Cite sources.
  2. Write an essay which relates Ridley's "The Origins of Virtue" to Raymond's "Homesteading the Noosphere".
    (Raymond's essay focuses on property and ownership customs of the open-source culture. Does Ridley's text support or refute Raymond's paper?)
    {Due April 5 }

Term Paper:

Write a term paper on Open Source. {Due in D2L before Fri. May 4, 5PM}
Discuss benefits and liabilities.
Include emphases on:
  • Definitions and distinctions (is it free software?)
  • motivations
  • origins and history: discuss some historical antecedants for open source both inside and outside the context of computing
  • Social context: open source is useful only in the context of a social network.
    Explore the synergy between open source and social networks.
    Explore as well the implications for numerous contexts of application and analysis
  • Methods & tools of analysis: Explain how open source can facilitate and improve processes of deliberation and information gathering in the service of ethical analysis.
  • Discuss open source in the context of two or more prominent ethical frameworks
  • Professional & ethical responsibilities: how does the open source process correlate to tenets of relevant codes of ethics?
  • Risks (reduces or increases? how? for whom?)
  • Privacy & civil liberties: Explain how open source can be applied to help protect privacy and other civil liberties in several contexts including cryptography and voting integrity.
  • Computer crime: explore the application of open source to the commission, to the detection, and to the prevention of computer crime.
  • Intellectual property
    • describe licence issues, commonalities and differences, e.g. GNU (full and lesser), LaTeX, etc.
    • describe copyright vs. trade secrets, patents, trademarks (what is fair use?)
  • economic issues w.r.t.
    • programmers
    • businesses
    • consumers and society
    • use value, market value, and monopoly value
    • contrast the open source movement to the free software movement
  • open source alternatives to proprietary standards and formats, e.g.,
    • sound files
    • graphics files (lossy and lossless)
  • recent open source issues with significant public exposure (lots of newsprint)
  • summary in context of ethics and ethical obligations
Use and reference any sources including:
  • relevant Web links I have provided below;
  • any other book, magazine, or Web reference you find relevant.
Provide a table of contents and a bibliography
